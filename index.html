IPv4 & IPv6 Duality and BGP Anomalies

4.8.2016
  A fellow group member and I have decided to resume work on the duality project we began in January. Instead of continuing to explore network topologies at the router level, we would like to concentrate our efforts on learning more about the nature of BGP disruptions, which are occurring on a grander scale. As explained in my portfolio, we have access to a large amount of archived RIB table data. Our system currently takes an average of six minutes to process routing tables for both IPv4 and IPv6, then outputs path information for a single ASN. Being that large autonomous systems have several ASN's, our first goal is to parallelize RIB table processing in an attempt to broaden our understanding.
